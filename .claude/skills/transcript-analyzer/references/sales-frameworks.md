# Sales Frameworks Reference

## Cole Gordon's 7 Beliefs
Every prospect needs these 7 beliefs to buy. Map extracted data to which beliefs are present, missing, or weak.

1. Pain — A problem or unfulfilled desire exists
2. Doubt — They can't fix it alone (or it would cost more in time/energy/money to do so)
3. Cost — The cost of inaction over time exceeds the cost of investing
4. Desire — There's a compelling payoff if the problem gets fixed
5. Money — They have the resources AND willingness to invest
6. Support — People around them support the decision
7. Trust — They believe the methodology is the simultaneous explanation of why past attempts failed and why this is different

## NEPQ Principles (Jeremy Miner)
- Questions create more conviction than statements
- The prospect must feel the pain themselves — the rep's job is to help them articulate it, not to tell them they have a problem
- Connecting questions: tie surface pain to the deeper emotional/identity cost
- Consequence questions: what happens if nothing changes?
- Commitment questions: get micro-agreements throughout the call

## Hormozi CLOSER Framework
C — Clarify why they're there
L — Label their problem
O — Overview their past attempts and why they failed
S — Sell the vacation (the outcome), not the plane (the mechanism)
E — Explain away their concerns using the simultaneous explanation
R — Reinforce their decision after they say yes

## Pain Cycle Methodology
The pain cycle is the chronological story of a prospect's failed attempts. It is the most powerful copy structure available because it mirrors the prospect's exact lived experience. Structure: Current pain → First attempt → Why it failed → Second attempt → Why it failed → Emotional cost of repeated failure → Inflection point → Why they're on this call.
