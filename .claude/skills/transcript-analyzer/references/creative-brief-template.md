# Creative Brief Template — Phase 3

The brief is NOT copy. It is the strategic foundation a copywriter will use to write ads, VSLs, sales pages, and scripts.

## 1. Audience Snapshot
- One-paragraph psychographic portrait written in plain language
- Current emotional state in one sentence
- #1 desire in one sentence
- #1 fear in one sentence

## 2. Pain Arsenal
- Top 5 pains ranked by emotional weight with verbatim language for each
- The "killer pain" — the single pain that, if agitated properly, would move this prospect to action on its own

## 3. Desire Arsenal
- Top 5 desires ranked by motivational pull
- The "North Star desire" — the one outcome they'd pay almost anything for

## 4. Objection Map
Categorize every objection as:
- Financial (can't afford it, too expensive, need to move funds)
- Uncertainty (not sure it'll work, how is this different, I've been burned)
- Spouse/Partner/Authority (need to talk to someone)
- Timing (not the right time, too busy, need to think about it)

For each objection: verbatim language used + which of Cole Gordon's 7 Beliefs is missing (Pain, Doubt, Cost, Desire, Money, Support, Trust)

## 5. Failed Solution Narrative
A chronological "pain cycle" composite — the typical journey of this prospect before they got on the call. Written as a story: "First they tried X, then Y happened, so they tried Z, and that failed because..."

## 6. Hook Bank (10 minimum)
Generate at least 10 hook concepts (not full scripts — core hook idea in 1-2 sentences each). Categorize by angle:
- Pain-lead hooks (agitate a specific pain)
- Contrarian hooks (challenge a belief they hold)
- Curiosity hooks (open a loop about something they don't know)
- Proof hooks (lead with a result or case study angle)
- Identity hooks (speak to who they are or want to become)

For each hook, note which transcript insight it was derived from.

## 7. Simultaneous Explanation Angles
3-5 angles that explain why everything the prospect tried before failed AND why this solution is different. Each is a 2-3 sentence thesis. Root each in actual failed attempts extracted from transcripts.

## 8. Recommended Awareness & Sophistication Level
Based on extraction, recommend the most likely awareness level (Unaware → Most Aware) and sophistication level (1-5). Justify with specific evidence from transcripts.
