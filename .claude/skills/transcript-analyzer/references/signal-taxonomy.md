# Signal Taxonomy Reference

## Buying Signal Types
- Explicit verbal commitment: "I want to move forward," "how do we get started"
- Logistics questions: asking about onboarding, timelines, what happens after they sign
- Future-pacing: "so if I did this, would I be able to..." — they are mentally living in the outcome
- Objection reduction: they start answering their own objections before the rep does
- Language mirroring: they start using the rep's terminology back at them
- Guarantee questions: asking about guarantees signals they're past "should I" and into "how do I protect myself"
- Tonality shift: moving from flat/guarded to engaged/animated — note this even if the words don't explicitly signal it

## Objection Categories
- Financial: "I can't afford it right now," "that's more than I expected," "I need to move some money around"
- Uncertainty: "how is this different from what I tried before," "I've been burned," "what if it doesn't work"
- Authority: "I need to talk to my wife/partner/business partner first"
- Timing: "I need to think about it," "now isn't the right time," "I'm too busy right now"

## Micro-Commitment Markers
Small agreements during the call that build toward the close:
- Agreeing the current situation is painful
- Agreeing they've tried to fix it and failed
- Agreeing the cost of inaction is real
- Agreeing they want change
- Agreeing the methodology makes sense
Each micro-commitment is a belief being installed. Track them — a prospect who gives 4+ micro-commitments before the close is highly likely to buy.

## Emotional Weight Scale
1 — Mentioned once, flat tone, no elaboration
2 — Mentioned with some detail, mild frustration
3 — Returned to unprompted, noticeable emotional charge
4 — Extended elaboration, personal/family impact mentioned, strong language
5 — Highly charged, repeated multiple times, visible distress or urgency in language
