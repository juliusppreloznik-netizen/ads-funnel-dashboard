# Extraction Framework — Phase 1

Before extracting anything: read the entire transcript first. Skip the first 2-3 minutes — this is rapport-building and logistics, not intelligence. Only extract from the discovery, pitch, and objection-handling sections.

If you cannot find a genuine example of a category, return an empty array. Never fabricate. Never stretch a quote to fit a category.

## 1. Pain Points

A pain point is a specific, emotionally-charged problem the prospect is currently experiencing that is causing real frustration, lost revenue, or lost time. It must be something they are actively suffering from — not mentioned in passing or as context.

Extract:
- Surface-level pain: what they openly state is wrong
- Root-cause pain: the deeper emotional, financial, or identity cost beneath the surface pain
- Verbatim language: exact words, slang, cadence — never clean it up
- Emotional weight: rate 1-5 based on repetition, language intensity, time spent on it, personal/family connection

Detection signals:
- Words: "struggling," "frustrated," "burned," "stuck," "can't figure out," "overwhelmed," "burnt out," "on the verge of," "inconsistent," "unpredictable," "wasting money"
- Situation described as getting worse over time
- Language becomes more emotional: longer sentences, more detail, personal stories
- Prospect connects business problem to personal or family impact
- Prospect describes feeling trapped or out of options

NOT pain points: small talk, logistics, apologies for being late, how they found you, background context

## 2. Buying Signals

A buying signal is a statement where the prospect reveals they are actively looking for a solution, have budget, have a timeline, or express genuine enthusiasm. It must indicate forward momentum or desire to change.

Extract:
- Explicit signals: verbal commitment, agreement, asks about next steps, pricing, timelines
- Implicit signals: future-pacing ("so if I did this..."), detailed implementation questions, mirroring rep's language
- Commitment micro-moments: agreeing to smaller asks during the call
- Signal timing: note WHERE in the call (early, mid-discovery, post-pitch, during close)

Detection signals:
- "That makes sense" / "That's exactly what I need" / "How does that work?"
- Asking about logistics: pricing, timelines, onboarding, what happens next
- Future-pacing: "So if I did this..." / "Would I be able to..."
- Asking about other clients' results
- Reducing their own objections before the rep does ("I know money shouldn't be the issue...")
- Asking about guarantees (signals they're past "should I" and into "how do I protect myself")
- Tonality shift from skeptical/guarded to engaged/curious

NOT buying signals: generic compliments, polite agreement, casual curiosity

## 3. Business Objectives

Specific, measurable goals the prospect stated they want to achieve. Must be concrete, not vague.

Real: "I want to go from $40k to $100k/month in the next 6 months"
Not real: "I want to grow my business"

Extract:
- Revenue goals (stated or implied)
- Client volume goals
- Lifestyle/freedom goals tied to the business
- Timeline urgency (deadline, financial cliff, seasonal window)
- Growth model they envision (scale a team, stay solo, build to sell)

## 4. Perceived Obstacles

What THEY believe is standing in their way — this is different from what's actually in the way.

Extract:
- Resource constraints (money, time, knowledge, team)
- Belief constraints ("I've tried this before," "my market is different," "ads don't work for credit repair")
- External blame patterns (economy, platform changes, bad agencies, bad leads)
- Internal blame patterns (procrastination, shiny object syndrome — only if they admit these)

Detection signals:
- "I've tried that before" / "I've been burned" / "How is this different?"
- "My market is different" / "That won't work for..."
- "I don't have the time/money/team to..."
- "I need to talk to my spouse/partner"
- "I need to think about it"
- Any language that externalizes blame

## 5. Past Failed Attempts

Every solution, agency, coach, course, strategy, or tool they tried before.

For each attempt extract: what they tried, how long, what went wrong, how much they spent, how it made them feel.

Detection signals:
- Listen for the pain cycle sequence: "What have you tried?" → "How long?" → "Did it work?" → "What else?"
- Prospect lists agencies, coaches, courses, or tools by name
- Specific dollar amounts spent on failed solutions
- Emotional aftermath: disappointment, skepticism, feeling scammed
- Timeline: "For two years I was doing X..."
- Inflection point: "That's when I decided to look into something else"
